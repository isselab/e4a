Agentic software engineering meets industry.

E4A aims to establish a European community around agentic software engineering, driven by real and scalable industrial practice. The main reason to participate is the exchange of actual practices: what teams are trying, what is working, what is failing, and what can realistically scale. The meeting brings together engineering leaders, platform and productivity teams, tool builders, and applied researchers to move agentic SE from lab demos toward dependable software delivery in Europe.

How It Works

A small, curated meeting built for honest exchange.

E4A borrows from proven industry summit formats: keep the room focused, let participants shape the agenda, and turn concrete experience into shared European priorities.

Participant-shaped agenda

Before the meeting, participants vote on discussion topics so focus sessions reflect current industrial challenges.

Practice-first sessions

Sessions start with short 3-5 minute experience statements, followed by moderated discussion among the room.

What to bring

Each participant should bring one concrete practice, one failure mode or concern, and one open question for Europe.

What you get

Peer practices, adoption patterns, failure modes, contacts for collaboration, and input into a European vision report.

Focus Areas

From agent demos to dependable software delivery.

The agenda is centered on practice exchange: concrete industrial questions, real adoption experience, where agentic SE works today, where it does not yet work, and what Europe should build next.

Production

From lab demos to delivery

How to create agentic systems that become reliable parts of real production software delivery.

Methods

Using agentic SE tools

How teams use agentic methods and tools for coding, review, testing, maintenance, and knowledge work.

Practice

Where it works, where it fails

Exchange actual practices, adoption patterns, failure modes, and lessons learned from industrial settings.

Europe

Digital sovereignty and competitiveness

How Europe can compete, stay independent in critical capabilities, and shape agentic SE infrastructure.

Assurance

Evaluation and trust

Benchmarks, observability, failure modes, and evidence for when an agentic setup is safe to rely on.

Governance

Control, compliance, accountability

Policies, approvals, traceability, and responsibility boundaries when agents act inside delivery pipelines.

Preliminary Program

Two days of keynotes, practitioner panels, practice snapshots, and community building.

The exact timing will evolve with invited speakers and participants. The current structure keeps both days centered on exchanging actual practices: morning keynotes, participant-shaped panels, short practice snapshots, lunch, another panel, synthesis, mingle, and dinner.
