A working summit on agentic software engineering in industry.

Agentic software engineering uses AI agents that plan and carry out development work, such as writing, reviewing, testing, and maintaining code, with human oversight. E4A is an invite-only, two-day summit on how this is playing out in European industry. Participants exchange what they are doing in practice: which methods and tools they use, what works, what fails, and what does not yet scale. The room brings together engineering leaders, platform and developer-productivity teams, tool builders, and applied researchers. Discussion runs under the Chatham House Rule, and the summit works toward a shared white paper: a vision for agentic software engineering in Europe.

Why Attend

We bring together engineering leaders, platform and developer-productivity teams, and tool builders, alongside a few applied researchers. Under the Chatham House Rule, you can speak candidly and hear what peers are really doing.

Preliminary Program

Two days of experience reports and open discussion.

Each day opens with two morning keynotes, followed by experience-report talks and discussion sessions. Before the summit, participants answer a short survey to propose concrete topics, so the agenda reflects the challenges practitioners actually face. Talks are short and practice-focused; most of the time is open exchange. Timing is preliminary and will be finalized with invited speakers and participants.

Themes we expect to discuss:

  • Moving agentic systems from lab demos into production delivery
  • Using agentic SE methods and tools in practice
  • Where it works today and where it does not
  • European competitiveness and digital sovereignty
  • Trust, quality, and evaluation
  • Collaboration between industry and research

Senior practitioners working on agentic SE in industry, plus a few applied researchers. One participant per organization.

What language?
