E4A Summit on Agentic Software Engineering

The software world is changing radically. New agentic development methods and tools use AI agents that plan and carry out development work, such as writing, reviewing, testing, and maintaining code, with human oversight. E4A is a working summit to exchange practices on deploying and using agentis SE in industry. We discuss methods and tools used, what works, what fails, and what does not yet scale. We aim to bring together practitioners, tool builders, and researchers. Our discussions follow the Chatham House Rule, we work towards a report of practices and a vision, strengthening the European community in this area.

Benefits

The Summit Targets Practitioners

We bring together engineering leaders, platform and developer-productivity teams, and tool builders, alongside applied researchers. Hear what peers are really doing and see where you stand. Compare your agentic SE adoption against peers in other companies and sectors. Get early access to research by direct exchange with researchers working on agentic SE, and a first look at emerging methods and evidence. Shape the agenda by proposing opics in a pre-summit survey..

Preliminary Program

Two days of experience reports and open discussion.

Each day opens with two morning keynotes, followed by experience-report talks and discussion sessions. Before the summit, participants answer a short survey to propose concrete topics, so the agenda reflects the challenges practitioners actually face. Talks are short and practice-focused; most of the time is open exchange. Timing is preliminary and will be finalized with invited speakers and participants.

Themes we expect to discuss:

  • Moving agentic systems from lab demos into production delivery
  • Using agentic SE methods and tools in practice
  • Where it works today and where it does not
  • European competitiveness and digital sovereignty
  • Trust, quality, and evaluation
  • Collaboration between industry and research
